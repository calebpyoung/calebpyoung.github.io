Prison Conditions

America's jails and prisons are some of the biggest coronavirus hotspots.

Those behind bars are unable to follow health guidelines

Social distancing is impossible when prison beds are as few as three feet apart. Following sanitation protocol is difficult when some states won't even provide hand sanitizer. The effects are clear. An Ohio prison is the state's biggest coronavirus hotspot. The infection rate at Rikers Island in New York City is nearly 38 times the national average. And coronavirus is "tearing through" the Illinois correctional system.With few options available to inmates, the spread is likely to continue. You can help us protect prisoners, correctional officers, and their broader communities by donating to our sanitation supply fund.

Cook County Jail

We deliver supplies to the country's biggest cluster of coronavirus victims.

Cook County Jail needs your help

  • The jail is the nation's largest-known source of the coronavirus.
  • More than 500 inmates and more than 100 staff members have tested positive for the virus.
  • The jail holds many nonviolent offenders and many people who have not been convicted of a crime.

NPEP provides an education to those incarcerated in Cook County Jail. Now, we need your help to keep the population safe. Your donation helps us deliver crucial sanitation supplies.

Our Work

We are delivering supplies to fight the epidemic.

The Northwestern Prison Education Program, a post-secondary education program affiliated with Northwestern University, has been working to combat the spread of COVID-19 in Illinois correctional centers. To date, NPEP has delivered more than $21,000 worth of supplies to Stateville Correctional Center, Logan Correctional Center, Cook County Jail, and the Juvenile Detention Center in St. Charles. That's more than 12,000 bars of soap and more than 300 gallons of hand sanitizer. But we are hoping to do more. Please give today to help us with this important work.
